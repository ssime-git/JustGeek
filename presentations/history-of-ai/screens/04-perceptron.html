CHAPTER 04

1958 · Cornell University, New York

The Perceptron and
the First Hype Cycle

Cornell psychologist Frank Rosenblatt built a machine that could adjust itself — the first neural network. The press declared it conscious. The reality was a coin flip.

FRANK ROSENBLATT · 1928–1971
THE MARK I PERCEPTRON — HOW IT WORKED
The radical part: if the answer was wrong, the machine adjusted its own weights. It wasn't following a fixed program — it was learning. After 50 trials, it could tell triangles from squares. That loop — predict, measure error, adjust — is the same loop every neural network uses today.
THE HYPE CYCLE — SAME AS IT EVER WAS
"The Navy revealed the embryo of an electronic computer today that it expects will be able to walk, talk, see, write, reproduce itself and be conscious of its existence."
New York Times · July 8, 1958
The Headlines Said
Walk. Talk.
See. Write.
Be conscious.
Navy funding. Front page. Detachment from engineering reality.
The Machine Actually Did
50–60%
accuracy
Distinguishing triangles from squares. A coin flip is 50%. Real advance. Wildly oversold.
A real technical advance can still be wildly oversold. The field keeps relearning this lesson.
