The winters hadn't ended because the problems were solved. They ended because three missing ingredients finally arrived — at scale — at the same time: data, compute, method.

THE PEOPLE WHO DIDN'T GIVE UP — 1980s – 2012

While the field moved on to expert systems and then into a second winter (1987–1993), a small group of researchers kept working on neural networks when almost no one else did. They were ignored, underfunded, and repeatedly told they were chasing a dead end. Three of them would share the 2018 Turing Award — computing's highest honor. A fourth created the dataset that proved them right.

Geoffrey Hinton
Backprop (1986). Boltzmann machines. AlexNet (2012). Godfather of deep learning. Turing Award 2018.
Yann LeCun
Convolutional networks (1989). Handwriting recognition. Chief AI Scientist at Meta. Turing Award 2018.
Yoshua Bengio
Language models, attention mechanisms, deep learning theory. Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms. Turing Award 2018.
Fei-Fei Li
Created ImageNet (2009) — 1.2M labeled images, the dataset that made AlexNet possible. Stanford AI Lab co-director.

The architecture wasn't new — convolutional layers and backprop existed since the 1980s. What changed was scale: 60M parameters, 1.2M training images, 2 GPUs running in parallel. The lesson was unmistakable: depth + data + compute worked.
2017
Transformers — "Attention Is All You Need"
Google Brain · Vaswani et al. · Parallel processing of sequences
The Transformer replaced recurrence with self-attention. Every token could attend to every other token in parallel — training became massively faster. Designed for translation, it became the universal architecture: BERT, GPT, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion. All descendants of this one paper.

ChatGPT was not a fundamental research breakthrough. The architecture was five years old. What was new was the interface: a chat window that made large language models accessible to anyone. It reached 100 million users in two months — the fastest consumer software adoption in history. The arc from Turing's Bombe had crossed into public life.
