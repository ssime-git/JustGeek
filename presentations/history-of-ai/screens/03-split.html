CHAPTER 03

1956 – 1969 · The Fundamental Split

Rules or Learning:
The First Civil War

The early argument in AI was not over whether machine intelligence was possible. It was over what intelligence was. Two men personified the disagreement. Neither would yield for a decade.

THE TWO CAMPS
Symbolic AI
Marvin Minsky
"Intelligence is reason. Give the machine enough rules and it will think."
VS
Neural Networks
Frank Rosenblatt
"Intelligence is adaptation. Let the machine learn from examples, like a brain."
SIDE BY SIDE COMPARISON
Symbolic AI Dimension Neural Networks
Top-down Direction Bottom-up
Logic & rules Method Learning from data
Explicit knowledge Knowledge Discovered patterns
If-then trees Structure Weighted connections
Brittle Failure mode Opaque
Interpretable Strength Flexible
Programmer must know the rules Limit Needs data to learn from

That divide still echoes today. Every argument about large language models, explainability, and alignment is a descendant of this original split: do we get intelligence by specifying it, or by letting systems learn it?

"Intelligence is reason."
Minsky camp · Specify the rules, build the knowledge base
"Intelligence is adaptation."
Rosenblatt camp · Train on examples, let patterns emerge
